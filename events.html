WISA 2022

After the border reopened, WISA hosted an orientation day to welcome over 140 students from 14 schools and 8 countries with visits to museums and sightseeing tours. In July, WISA responded to the racial tensions that arose during the pandemic by organizing a cross-cultural fundraiser, which featured performances by Asian and Polynesian groups. Through these initiatives, WISA has made significant strides towards achieving its goal of promoting racial unity and facilitating cultural exchange between international students and New Zealanders.

WISA 2021

WISA had a mixed year in 2021. The school year started with a Sports day at Wellington College, where the 2021 executive was selected. The new executive met with Education NZ representatives to discuss international students' experiences and attended a validation workshop for the National Ambassador program by Wellington NZ. WISA proceeded to organised a series of socialising events including a bowling night, a roller skating night, and a movie night. WISA finished the year by successfully launching its first merchandise line.

WISA 2020

WISA started the year with a successful movie night that had a "full house" of international students attending, including one who travelled all the way from Whanganui! The second event was a shared picnic and laser force activity, followed by a Korean BBQ buffet held in the same week. In August, we had a bowling event that brought everyone together for a fun night out. In October, we organized a sports day at Wellington College. The year ended with WISA teaming up with the Interact club for a quiz night fundraiser.

About Us

Wellington International Students' Association (WISA) is the largest umbrella group that connects international students across high schools in the region. We host numerous events to promote awareness of international culture to our diverse Wellington community, whilst assisting new students integrate into the cosmopolitan community.
